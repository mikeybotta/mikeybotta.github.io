About Me

TLDR:

My name is Mikey, and I'm 23 years old. I am currently living in the beautiful city of Austin, Texas. As someone who loves the outdoors, warm weather, and good food, ATX has proven itself to be an incredible choice. I have a Mini Aussiedoodle named Skye, who is just over a year old, although I doubt her puppy energy is ever going to fade. I really love pop music, especially (right now) Zara Larsson, Madison Beer, and Taylor Swift. I work as a tech recruiter at Austin Fraser, a pretty small recruiting agency based here in Austin. And that about wraps me up in a nutshell, but read on for an expansive journey through my life, my career, and my future.

and, my life story:

I was raised in a small town in northern New Jersey. Whereas the initial impression of NJ for many is of congested suburbs and the stinky Newark airport, my hometown, Sparta, is set up on a mountain, surrounded by woods, with clean air and bright stars in the night sky.

From there, I went down to South Carolina for college. USC was affordable, had a nice campus, and... that's about all of the positive things I have to say. Oh, and I was able to graduate in just three years! So I'm happy to have gone there but did not really enjoy the experience overall. I received my Bachelors of Science in Biological Sciences degree and graduated with magna cum laude honors in stride with my 3.82 GPA.

smallSUGAR was my first job, which I had from 2018 to 2019 while at USC. I loved our cafe, our pastries, and my team. I took a lot of pride in the business as I was one of the original first hires, and helped with everything from the Grand Opening to all the changes over the next two years. If I had to describe in one word what I did, I'd probably have to say 'Barista' but it really was a lot more than just that. Plus, over time, I assisted with training new staff as they were onboarded, and this was overall a really great learning experience as a leader. Plus, tons of free food and coffee!

I needed an escape from reality after my first year in college. I'd decided to go to summer camp. There's so many great camps out there, but I wanted to go all out. So I searched "most expensive camps in the US" and made my choices from there. I had some tough choices but I ended up choosing Camp Laurel in Maine, the deciding factor being the beauty of their lake. And I truly, honestly, genuinely, sincerely, had the best summer of my life. The type that I don't think I will ever beat. Now, it was hell while I was there... 7 weeks of 24/7 responsibility for the lives of 15 kids, and I was so burnt out by the end. But once camp ended, I realized how perfect the camp experience is for me. And I missed it, a lot.

...and then I came back again! Despite the intense burnout at the end of my first summer, I spent the next 10 months missing Camp Laurel, my kids, my co-counselors, being disconnected from technology, and having my meals cooked, laundry washed and folded, and no worries. Well, my cabin this summer was tough so after all I had tons of worries, 24/7. Not many counselors return year-to-year so I played a big role in helping mentor my fellow counselors, especially before the kids got there, but also throughout the whole summer. With my experience, I'd become a leader in our campus and provided a lot of guidance, support, and advice to our 30 counselors throughout the entire summer.

That last summer being really tough not dissaude me . I'd planned to come back for a 3rd summer in 2020, and all was good to go until COVID-19 cancelled all of my plans! I'll forever be bummed about that as it felt like a summer in my paradise was stolen from me. Realistically you can't be a camp counselor forever, and I'd just graduated.... but I made it work, couldn't not go back one more time-- and returned for another summer in 2021! I took on even more leadership roles this year, and almost felt like I could do this for the rest of my life.
